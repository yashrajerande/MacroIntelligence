01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP — down from 8.4% — is the kind of number that flatters on the cover page and disappoints on page three: PMI composite fell 240bps to 56.5, core sector decelerated to 2.3% from 4.7%, and capacity utilisation slipped to 74.8% — all pointing to a growth regime that is decelerating, not consolidating. GST at ₹2,00,064 cr is a genuine milestone, but two-wheeler sales flat at 13.4% and airline traffic at the 7th percentile historically suggest the informal economy — 85% of employment — is not sharing in the expansion. RBI's own GDP forecast at 6.9% versus the government's 7.6% FY estimate is a 70bps institutional disagreement that deserves more attention than it is receiving. India's growth is real but narrowing — concentrated in formal-sector services and government-adjacent capex, while the consumption base that sustains it is showing stress at the margins.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP SAAR at 0.7% — down from 4.4%, a 370bps collapse — is the most important global data point this week, yet S&P 500 at the 96th percentile and Nasdaq at the 100th percentile are pricing a soft landing with zero margin for error. US PMI composite at 50.3 (47th percentile) is one bad month from contraction; US core PCE ticking up to 3.1% from 3.0% means the Fed — balance sheet expanding to $6.675T despite flat rates at 3.625% — is caught between a slowing economy and sticky inflation. Iron ore surging 50% to $161.91/tonne on China stimulus expectations — while copper falls 0.7% to $5.718 — is a commodity market sending two contradictory signals simultaneously: hoarding fear versus demand destruction. Brent at $97.96 — every $10 adds $15bn to India's import bill — CAD widens 40bps — INR depreciates 2-3% — imported inflation feeds CPI with a two-quarter lag.

03
Liquidity Conditions

₹2,59,78 cr in equity MF net inflows and SIP accounts at 1.045 cr — up 5.3% MoM — represent a structural domestic liquidity floor that is deepening, but the composition is shifting: average SIP ticket size at ₹15,900 is rising while NFO collections collapsed to ₹4,979 cr (16th percentile), confirming that retail participation is systematic and SIP-driven, not speculative. FII equity net turning to -₹114 cr — first outflow in three months — with DII support at only ₹128 cr (22nd percentile) means domestic institutional buying is not yet absorbing the FII exit at scale. Corporate bond issuance at ₹9.9 lakh cr — up from ₹8.6 lakh cr — is crowding banks out of credit intermediation, compressing NIM potential even as CD ratio at 82.5% constrains deposit-funded lending. India VIX at 20.4 (16th percentile) is deceptively low given the structural credit stress — the market is not yet pricing the tail risk in the banking system's funding architecture.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Nifty 50 at 23,775 (76th percentile) and Bank Nifty at 54,822 (90th percentile) are pricing a benign credit cycle — a mispricing, given CD ratio at the 79th percentile and deposit growth structurally lagging credit by 280bps; NIM compression is the second-order effect that bank earnings models have not yet absorbed. Real estate is bifurcating with surgical clarity: office absorption rising to 29.9mn sqft with vacancy falling to 13.9% — driven by GCC demand, not traditional IT services — while residential sales fell to 101.7mn units from 109mn against launches of 126.3mn units, building 600,000 units of unsold inventory. Bengaluru rents up 7.5% to ₹100.6/sqft while Mumbai rents down 10.2% to ₹106/sqft — the divergence maps precisely onto GCC expansion in tech corridors versus traditional financial-sector office demand softening. Embassy REIT at ₹437 (78th percentile) is the cleaner expression of the GCC thesis than Nifty IT at 18x forward PE — 200bps above its five-year average — which is pricing a US tech recovery that US GDP at 0.7% does not support.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Ranked by probability × impact: first, fuel pass-through — Brent at $97.96 with fuel deflation narrowing to -3.78% from -4.01% means the CPI suppressor has perhaps two months of runway before administered price revision forces headline above 4.5%, closing the rate-cut window entirely and triggering a bond market reprice. Second, CD ratio crystallisation — at 82.5% for two consecutive months with NBFC credit accelerating to 9.5%, the risk is not a sudden crisis but a slow-motion NIM compression → earnings downgrade → Bank Nifty derating cycle that passive index flows will amplify on the way down. Third, US growth shock transmission — S&P 500 at the 96th percentile with US GDP at 0.7% is a valuation-growth disconnect that historically resolves violently; an equity correction of 10-15% in US markets would trigger FII outflows from India at a moment when DII support is at the 22nd percentile. Fourth, food inflation persistence — CFPI at 3.47% from 2.13% with FAO food index up 3 points to 128.5 creates a political economy constraint on RBI's flexibility that is underappreciated. Watch Friday's RBI liquidity operations and the weekly deposit growth print — if deposit growth does not accelerate toward 12% within the next four weeks, the credit-deposit gap becomes the dominant macro story for Q1 FY27.
