Executive Summary
▾
01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with PMI composite at 57 and GST breaching ₹2L crore for the first time constructs an irresistible headline — but three proxies undercut it: core sector at 2.3% (half its prior reading), airline passengers at a decade-low 7th percentile, and e-commerce GMV growth collapsing from 25-30% to 12.4%. RBI's own forecast of 6.9% sits 90bps below the print — that gap is either a deflator anomaly or a GVA-GDP statistical artefact, neither of which represents real output. Manufacturing PMI slipped to 53.9 from 56.9 while services held at 57.5 — India's growth engine is increasingly a services story, which employs fewer people per rupee of output than manufacturing. 7.8% is the number; whether it is the economy is a different question.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP at +0.7% SAAR — half the prior quarter's 1.4% — while core PCE ticked up to 3.1% from 3.0%: stagflation's shadow is lengthening even as equity markets price perfection, with S&P 500 at a 96th-percentile 6,782 and Nasdaq at an all-time high 22,635. Copper at a 94th-percentile $5.716/lb alongside iron ore surging 49.5% to $161.91 signals genuine industrial demand — most likely China-driven — that contradicts the US slowdown narrative and keeps global inflation risks alive. Brent's 11.5% collapse to $96.84 is India's single largest external gift this quarter — every $10 drop saves approximately $15bn on the import bill — but at $96.84, oil is still above the $85 level where India's CAD math becomes comfortable. DXY at 98.97 — a 46th-percentile reading — gives EM currencies room to breathe, but US core PCE re-accelerating forecloses Fed cuts and keeps the carry trade calculus unfavorable for India.

03
Liquidity Conditions

India VIX at 19.7 — down from 24.7, now at a 15th-percentile calm — masks the structural liquidity stress underneath: CD ratio at 83.04% with deposit growth at 10.8% against credit at 14.3% means banks are lending rupees they haven't yet collected. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore fell 3.7% MoM from ₹31,002 crore — the first meaningful deceleration in the retail savings pipeline — while FII equity outflows of ₹8,692 crore were almost exactly offset by DII inflows of ₹7,980 crore, a ₹712 crore net drain that the market absorbed without distress only because VIX compressed simultaneously. Corporate bond issuance at ₹5.5L crore in nine months versus ₹6.0L crore for all of FY22 signals that the wholesale funding market is being tapped urgently — refinancing pressure, not growth appetite, is driving issuance. If deposit growth doesn't recover to 12%+ within two quarters, the RBI faces a binary: tighten macro-prudential screws or watch the CD ratio breach 85% — a level with no modern precedent in India's banking system.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Nifty 50 at 23,997 (77th percentile) and Bank Nifty at 55,704 (92nd percentile) price a benign credit cycle — but Bank Nifty at the 92nd percentile while CD ratio sits at the 81st percentile is a valuation tension that resolves badly: NIM compression from deposit rate wars → earnings downgrades → derating, with the timing determined by when wholesale funding costs visibly spike. Real estate shows a clean divergence: residential launches up 2% to 126,265 units while sales fell 6.7% to 101,675 units — unsold inventory jumped to 600,000 units from 560 (a data anomaly in the prior figure, but directionally a supply overhang building). Office REITs tell a more interesting story — Embassy at 78th percentile, Mindspace at 79th percentile, absorption up 5.9% to 29.9mn sqft with vacancy falling to 13.9% — yet Nifty IT is flat YoY, suggesting the market doesn't believe the office demand is tech-sector-driven, or that it's GCC and BFSI absorption masking IT headcount stagnation. REITs offer a cleaner expression of the office recovery than IT equities at current valuations.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Ranked by probability × impact: first, CD ratio breach of 85% — probability high within two quarters if deposit growth stays at 10.8% — triggering macro-prudential tightening that reprices NBFC credit and compresses Bank Nifty earnings by an estimated 8-12%. Second, food inflation compounding — CFPI at 3.47% from 2.13% in one month, with FAO food index at 128.5 and rising — if the monsoon disappoints by even 5% below normal, RBI's 4.6% inflation forecast becomes a floor, not a ceiling, closing the rate-cut window entirely. Third, US stagflation feedback — GDP at 0.7% SAAR with core PCE at 3.1% keeps the Fed on hold, sustains US 10Y at 4.31%, and removes the EM carry trade tailwind that has kept INR stable at 92.59 despite ₹8,692 crore of FII equity outflows. Fourth, the airline passenger anomaly — 35.08mn at the 7th percentile is either a data artifact or the sharpest leading indicator of upper-income consumption stress in today's dataset; watch April traffic numbers as the single most important data point this week.
