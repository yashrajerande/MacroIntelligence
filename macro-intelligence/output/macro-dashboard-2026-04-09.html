01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with PMI composite at 56.5 is the headline — but core sector growth at 2.3% (down from 4.7%) and capacity utilisation slipping to 74.8% are the footnotes that matter more. GST at ₹2.0 lakh crore and two-wheeler sales at +13.4% confirm that consumption is genuinely broad-based — rural demand is not a laggard this cycle. What breaks this narrative: food CPI accelerating to 3.47% from 2.13% in a single month, with the FAO food index at 128.5 and Brent at $98.66 — the RBI's 4.6% inflation forecast revision (up from 2.1%) suggests the institution already sees the trapdoor. India's growth is real; its durability over the next two quarters is not.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP SAAR at 0.7% — halved from 1.4% prior quarter — while S&P 500 sits at the 96th percentile and Nasdaq at the 100th percentile is the most dangerous valuation-growth divergence in global markets today. Core PCE at 3.06% with the Fed funds rate at 3.625% means the Fed is already cutting into above-target inflation — a policy error in slow motion — while the Fed balance sheet expanding to $6.675 trillion signals stealth liquidity injection. China's composite PMI recovering to 50.25 from 49.25 and iron ore spiking 50% to $161.91/tonne suggest Beijing stimulus is landing in commodities, not consumption — a pattern that exports inflation to India's import bill. Copper declining to $5.698/tonne while iron ore surges is the demand-quality warning: infrastructure spending without industrial demand recovery.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity outflows at -₹8,692 crore — accelerating 2.6x from -₹3,295 crore prior month — are not being fully absorbed by DIIs at +₹7,979 crore (itself declining from ₹8,593 crore), leaving a net liquidity deficit in Indian equities. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore are declining from ₹31,002 crore, and NFO collections at ₹1,939 crore (6th percentile) signal that new retail investor acquisition has stalled — the SIP engine is running on existing accounts, not fresh capital. INR at 92.59 (84th percentile) with DXY rising to 99.04 means RBI is either burning reserves to defend the currency or accepting depreciation — both outcomes compress the real rate advantage that justified the easing cycle. Average SIP ticket size rising to ₹15,900 confirms affluent money is entering even as mass-market participation plateaus — a bifurcation that historically precedes index concentration risk.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Nifty at 23,775 (76th percentile) with Bank Nifty at 54,821 (90th percentile) — the banking index is priced for perfection at a moment when CD ratio at 83.04% and deposit growth at 10.8% structurally constrain NIM expansion. Office absorption at 29.9 mn sqft with vacancy compressing 330bps to 13.9% and Bengaluru rents spiking 9.5% to ₹100.6/sqft is a genuine real estate signal — but Nifty IT flat YoY confirms GCCs and non-IT corporates are the absorption driver, not traditional IT services at 18-20x FY27E with flat growth. Residential real estate affordability improving to 0.61 from 0.46 with home loan rates at 8.15% is structurally positive — but sales units declining to 101,675 from 108,970 while launches rise to 126,265 means unsold inventory at 600,000 units is building, not clearing. Embassy REIT at ₹437 (78th percentile) is the cleanest expression of the office absorption thesis — own the physical asset, not the IT services P&L.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Ranked by probability × impact: first, Brent at $98.66 (82nd percentile) — every $10 adds ~$15bn to India's annual import bill — CAD widens 40bps — INR depreciates 2-3% — imported inflation feeds CPI with a two-quarter lag — RBI forced to pause or reverse the easing cycle — corporate capex IRRs deteriorate — Nifty derates. Second, the CD ratio at 83.04% widening further — deposit rate wars → NIM compression → Bank Nifty earnings downgrades → passive outflows from index funds that are 30%+ weighted to financials. Third, US growth at 0.7% SAAR with equities at the 96th-100th percentile — a mean-reversion in S&P 500 triggers risk-off globally — FII outflows from India accelerate beyond the -₹8,692 crore February run-rate — INR breaks above 94. Watch Friday's RBI MPC minutes for any language shift on the inflation forecast revision from 2.1% to 4.6% — that 250bps internal revision is the single most important data point this week.
