India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with PMI composite at 56.5 is the headline; the subtext is a 240bps core sector deceleration to 2.3% and PMI manufacturing sliding to 53.9 — the expansion is real but narrowing at its industrial base. Vehicle sales across all three segments — PV at +26.1%, two-wheelers at +28.7%, CVs at +28.9% — confirm physical demand is genuine, not a statistical artifact, and GST at ₹2,00,640 cr crossing the ₹2 lakh cr threshold for the first time adds structural weight. Yet airline traffic down to 35.08 mn from 38.59 mn and e-commerce GMV growth halving to 12.4% from 25% signal that urban discretionary — the highest-margin, highest-multiplier segment — is decelerating. RBI's own GDP forecast at 6.9% vs market consensus at 7.6% is a 70bps gap that deserves more attention than it is receiving.

02
Global Macro Regime

0.7% SAAR US GDP — down from 4.4% — is the single most important global data point this week, and its collision with Nasdaq at an all-time high (22,635, 100th percentile) is a tension that historically resolves violently, not gradually. Brent at $98.26/bbl and WTI at $99.30 — with iron ore surging 50% to $161.91/tonne — suggest commodity markets are pricing China stimulus, not US recession; these two narratives cannot both be right simultaneously. DXY falling to 99.04 from 99.98 and US 10Y yields down 10bps to 4.24% signal growth fear is winning over inflation fear in bond markets — a Fed pivot narrative that, if wrong, reprices everything. Gold at $4,767/oz (84th percentile) is not an inflation hedge here; it is a geopolitical risk premium and a dollar-debasement trade running simultaneously.

03
Liquidity Conditions

CD ratio at 83.04% — the 81st percentile of its 10-year range — is the structural constraint on India's credit cycle: deposit growth at 10.8% cannot fund credit growth at 13.8% indefinitely without either deposit rate hikes (NIM compression) or credit rationing (growth slowdown). NBFC credit growth decelerating to 13% from 17% YoY is the first visible casualty — wholesale funding costs are rising faster than retail lending rates can absorb — and corporate bond issuance at ₹1,12,000 cr confirms banks are already rationing. FII equity outflows swinging from a ₹22,615 cr inflow to a ₹114 cr outflow in a single period — with DII inflows at only ₹128 cr — leaves INR at 92.59 (84th percentile) dependent on RBI intervention rather than organic capital flows. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 cr with average ticket size at ₹15,900/month (up 22% YoY) are the structural domestic liquidity anchor — but they cannot offset a sustained FII exit.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Nifty50 at 23,775 (76th percentile) and Bank Nifty at 54,822 (90th percentile) price in a benign credit cycle — a CD ratio at 83% and NBFC deceleration suggest Bank Nifty's premium is the most vulnerable single position in Indian equities today. Office absorption at 29.9 mn sqft with vacancy tightening to 13.9% from 17.2% and Bengaluru rents up 9% to ₹102/sqft is a GCC and BFSI story, not an IT services recovery — Nifty IT flat YTD while Embassy REIT at ₹437 (78th percentile) is the cleaner expression of this theme. Residential real estate shows a structural divergence: launches at 1,26,265 units outpacing sales at 1,01,675 units — unsold inventory rising to 6,00,000 units — even as affordability improves to 0.61 on lower home loan rates at 8.0%. Retail mall vacancy rising to 5.7% from 5.1% is the canary: if urban discretionary is plateauing, physical retail faces a demand ceiling that no vacancy statistic can paper over.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Ranked by probability × impact: first, a CD ratio breach above 85% — currently at 83.04% and rising — triggers macro-prudential tightening via risk weight increases on unsecured lending, which reprices ₹8-10 lakh cr of NBFC and bank credit simultaneously. Second, Brent sustaining above $98/bbl flips fuel inflation from -3.78% to positive by Q1 FY27 — every $10/bbl adds ~₹15,000 cr/month to India's import bill, widens CAD by 40bps, and feeds back into CPI with a two-quarter lag that RBI cannot cut through. Third, US GDP at 0.7% SAAR colliding with Nasdaq at its all-time high is a valuation dislocation that historically resolves with a 15-20% equity correction — an event that would trigger carry trade unwinding, INR depreciation beyond 94-95, and FII outflows that dwarf today's ₹114 cr exit. Watch Friday's RBI credit data release — specifically whether deposit growth accelerates above 11.5% or CD ratio crosses 84%; that single number determines whether the bear scenario is a tail risk or the base case.
