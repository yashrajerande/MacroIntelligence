Executive Summary

01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with a PMI Composite that has fallen 240bps in a single print to 56.5 — the growth regime is expansionary but decelerating faster than consensus has priced. Core sector at 2.3% YoY, down from 4.7%, is the tell: eight industries covering 40% of IIP are slowing simultaneously, and IIP capital goods' apparent acceleration to 12.5% is a base-effect artefact against a prior 4.3% print. GST at ₹2.006 lakh crore and vehicle sales — PV +26.1%, two-wheelers +28.68%, CVs +28.89% — confirm consumption is holding, but airline traffic at the 7th percentile and e-commerce GMV growth halving to 12.4% signal the goods-services divergence is widening, not narrowing.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP SAAR at 0.7% — down from 4.4% — is the single most important number in today's global data set, and the market has chosen to ignore it: S&P 500 at 6,886 (98th percentile), Nasdaq at an all-time high of 23,184. US Core PCE has ticked up to 3.1% from 3.0% while the Fed sits at 3.625% with its balance sheet expanding to $6,675bn — a combination that historically precedes either a policy error or a repricing of the long end. Copper at $6.008/lb (100th percentile — all-time high) diverges sharply from Brent's softness at $98.11: copper is supply-constrained and China-stimulus-driven, not a global demand signal — the two cannot both be right for long.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net flows flipped from +₹22,615 crore to -₹114 crore in a single period — not a rout, but the directional reversal matters more than the quantum when DII absorption has shrunk to ₹128 crore. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore are down from ₹31,002 crore, and SIP account growth is at the 2nd percentile historically — the retail SIP engine is not adding new participants at scale, it is deepening wallet share among existing affluent investors (SIP AUM at ₹16.64 lakh crore, 82nd percentile). INR at 93.46 — 85th percentile, a 350bps depreciation from 90.50 three months ago — with gold in INR terms at an all-time high of ₹2,000/gram confirms the currency weakness is structural, not noise; RBI's defence is quiet but the FX reserve cushion is being tested.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Bank Nifty at 55,605 (92nd percentile) is making a structural claim — that CD ratio stress, NBFC deceleration, and NIM compression are already priced — that the data does not support: corporate bond issuance at the 7th percentile signals credit rationing, not credit health. Residential real estate is the most acute divergence in today's data: launches at an all-time high of 126,265 units against sales at an all-time low of 101,675 units — a 5% sales collapse while supply surges 2% — with unsold inventory rising to 600,000 units and the affordability index at its worst-ever reading of 0.61. Commercial real estate tells the opposite story: office vacancy has fallen from 17.2% to 13.9%, retail mall vacancy is at the 11th percentile (5.7%), and Bengaluru rents are up 9% to ₹102 — the residential-commercial bifurcation is the sharpest since 2015.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Three risks, ranked by probability × impact: first, CD ratio at 83.04% forcing macro-prudential action — RBI has used risk-weight increases on unsecured lending before (November 2023), and the incentive to act again is higher now that NBFC credit growth has already decelerated from 17% to 13%; a risk-weight circular on specific segments would reprice Bank Nifty at the 92nd percentile faster than any rate move. Second, US Core PCE at 3.1% with GDP SAAR at 0.7% is a stagflationary configuration — if the Fed is forced to hold or hike into a slowdown, the DXY at 98.4 could spike, INR at 93.46 has no natural floor, and every $10 on Brent widens India's CAD by ~40bps while the currency amplifies imported inflation with a two-quarter lag. Third, residential real estate inventory at 600,000 units with affordability at its worst-ever level — home loan disbursements at ₹5.5 lakh crore are holding, but a 50bps rate reversal would expose the inventory overhang immediately; watch Friday's RBI MPC minutes for any language shift on the neutral rate.
