Global Macro Regime

S&P 500 at 6,967 (99th percentile) and Nasdaq at 23,639 (all-time high) while US GDP decelerates to 0.7% SAAR from 4.4% — a 370bps deceleration — is the central contradiction in global markets today. US PMI Composite at 50.3 is one bad print from contraction, US core PCE ticked up to 3.1% from 3.0%, and the Fed has not moved at 3.625% — the equity market is pricing a soft landing that the activity data does not yet confirm. Copper at an all-time high of $6.09/lb alongside iron ore surging to $161.91/tonne from $107.05 signals that industrial metals markets are pricing a construction and manufacturing recovery — a different story from the services-led US slowdown. China GDP at 4.5% (down from 5.0%) with CPI at 1.3% (up from 0.2%) suggests the deflationary pressure is easing, which is the most plausible explanation for the metals surge.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net flows at -₹114 crore — negative for three consecutive weeks — against DII absorption of just ₹128 crore means domestic institutional buying is not providing the buffer it did in FY24, when DII flows routinely offset FII exits by a factor of five. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore are down 3.7% sequentially despite account growth to 10.45 crore — the average ticket size is falling, which means the marginal SIP rupee is coming from smaller, more rate-sensitive savers who will pause before the affluent cohort does. Corporate bond issuance at ₹1,12,000 crore at the 7th percentile signals banks are rationing credit and forcing corporates to capital markets at a cost — this is the early-stage transmission of CD ratio stress into real economy funding. INR at 93.46 (85th percentile weakness) with DXY actually down to 98.1 means RBI intervention is the only explanation for the rupee not weakening further — a defence that depletes reserves and constrains the rate-cut option simultaneously.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Nifty at 23,843 (76th percentile) with Bank Nifty at 55,605 (92nd percentile) is a divergence that encodes a specific risk: if CD ratio at 83.04% triggers macro-prudential tightening via risk-weight increases on unsecured lending — the RBI's preferred tool — Bank Nifty's premium to Nifty compresses sharply, and passive index rebalancing amplifies the move. Real estate presents a cleaner picture: home loan rates at 8.0% (down from 9.1%) have pushed the affordability index to 0.61, office vacancy in Bengaluru has fallen to 13.9% from 17.2%, and retail mall vacancy at 5.7% is near cycle lows — the commercial real estate cycle is in better shape than the equity market's real estate index suggests. Residential sales at 1,01,675 units are down from 1,08,970 while launches at 1,26,265 are up — unsold inventory at 6,00,000 units is rising, the classic leading indicator of developer margin pressure in 12–18 months. India VIX at 20.5 (up from 18.85) while US VIX at 18.36 (13th percentile) signals that domestic equity is pricing more uncertainty than global peers — not irrational given the CD ratio and INR pressures.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Ranked by probability × impact: first, CD ratio stress cascading — at 83.04% with deposit growth at 10.8% and credit at 13.8%, any RBI macro-prudential action on risk weights triggers NBFC funding costs higher, credit growth slows to single digits, and the consumption story that underpins the 7.8% GDP print loses its primary transmission mechanism. Second, the Brent floor — at $95.14, every $5 move higher adds roughly $7.5bn to India's annual import bill — CAD widens 20bps — INR depreciates — imported inflation feeds CPI with a two-quarter lag, and RBI's 4.6% inflation forecast becomes optimistic. Third, US growth deceleration contagion — US GDP at 0.7% SAAR with PMI at 50.3 is one quarter from a technical slowdown; if S&P 500 at the 99th percentile corrects 10%, FII outflows from India — already negative — accelerate, INR breaks 94, and the RBI's intervention calculus changes entirely. Watch Thursday's RBI liquidity operation size — if the overnight rate drifts above repo at 5.25%, the deposit war has begun.
