01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with a PMI Composite that fell to 56.5 from 58.9 and core sector growth at 2.3% — down from 4.7% — is an economy where the headline flatters the internals. IIP capital goods at 12.5% YoY looks like investment momentum until you note that cement and steel dispatch show no corresponding acceleration, making the print a base-effect artefact rather than a capex cycle. Airline passengers at the 7th decile percentile of the last decade while PV sales surge 26.1% YoY is the sharpest single-frame portrait of India's bifurcated economy: the affluent are buying cars and homes, the mass consumer is grounding flights. GVA growth, which strips out the statistical discrepancy that inflates GDP by 150-200bps in strong quarters, is the number to watch — not the 7.8% press release.

02
Global Macro Regime

S&P 500 at 7,022 and Nasdaq at 24,016 — both at the 100th percentile of the last decade — with US VIX at 18.17 (12th percentile) is the definition of a fully-priced, low-fear equity market. US GDP SAAR collapsed to 0.7% from 4.4% prior — a 370bps deceleration — while US core PCE ticked up to 3.1%, meaning the Fed is navigating stagflation-lite with no rate cuts priced and no rate hikes warranted. Copper at the 100th percentile all-time high and iron ore up 52% from prior month signal that commodity markets are pricing a global industrial recovery that PMI data — Global Composite at 51.0, US at 50.3 — does not yet confirm.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net flows flipped to -₹114 crore from +₹22,615 crore prior — a reversal of ₹22,729 crore in a single period — while DII absorption at +₹128 crore is at the 22nd percentile, too thin to absorb sustained FII selling. INR at 93.46 — the weakest level in the dataset — with Brent at $94.93 creates a compounding import bill: every $10 on Brent adds approximately $15bn to India's annual oil import cost, widening CAD by 20-30bps and pressuring INR further. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore provide a domestic liquidity floor, but the ₹1,12,000 crore corporate bond issuance spike — 7th percentile historically — signals that credit rationing is already forcing disintermediation away from banks.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Bank Nifty at 56,302 — the 93rd percentile — making new highs while CD ratio sits at 83.04% and deposit growth trails credit by 300bps is a divergence that historically resolves via a macro-prudential circular on risk weights, not via deposit growth catching up. Real estate shows a clean split: home loan disbursements at ₹5,50,000 crore (76th percentile) with average rates down to 8% from 9.1% are driving launches to 1,26,265 units, but sales fell to 1,01,675 units while unsold inventory jumped to 6,00,000 units — the supply-demand gap is widening even as affordability improves. Office absorption at 29.9 msf with vacancy falling to 13.9% from 17.2% is the one clean positive in the real estate complex — commercial is tightening while residential is accumulating overhang.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Ranked by probability × impact: first, Brent sustaining above $95 — at current trajectory, two months above this level widens CAD by 40bps, pressures INR past 94, and imports inflation into CPI with a two-quarter lag that arrives precisely when RBI may be cutting. Second, CD ratio at 83.04% triggering a macro-prudential response — the RBI has used risk-weight increases on unsecured lending before (November 2023); a repeat targeting housing or NBFC wholesale funding would reprice Bank Nifty from the 93rd percentile. Third, the consumer bifurcation deepening — e-commerce GMV at 12.4% YoY (15th percentile) and airline passengers at the 7th percentile suggest mass-market demand is already contracting, and if two-wheeler sales — at 28.68% YoY today — revert, the GST headline will follow with a one-quarter lag. Watch Friday's RBI liquidity data and the next fortnightly CD ratio print — those two numbers will determine whether the bear scenario is a tail risk or the base case.
