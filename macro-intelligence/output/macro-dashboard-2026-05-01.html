Executive Summary

01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with PMI Composite at 56.5 is the headline; the honest read is a two-speed economy splitting in real time. Core sector decelerated 240bps to 2.3% — cement, steel, electricity, coal are the economy's circulatory system, and they are slowing — while IIP capital goods at 12.5% flatters by measuring orders, not throughput. Airline passengers at 35.08mn (7th percentile, decade low) and e-commerce GMV growth halved to 12.4% from 25% confirm that affluent discretionary demand — the segment that drives services PMI to 57.5 — is already turning. GST at ₹2,00,640 crore and two-wheeler sales at 28.68 lakh units hold the mass economy together, but the leading indicators are pointing one quarter ahead of where the consensus is looking.

02
Global Macro Regime

Copper at $6.03 — an all-time high, 100th percentile — is pricing a global industrial expansion that US GDP at 0.7% SAAR and US PMI Composite at 50.3 (47th percentile) explicitly contradict. S&P 500 at 7,209 and Nasdaq at 24,892 (both 100th percentile) with US VIX at 16.89 (11th percentile) suggest markets have priced a soft landing; US core PCE ticking up to 3.1% from 3.0% with the Fed funds rate steady at 3.625% means the Fed has no room to validate that optimism. Iron ore's 51% surge to $161.91 alongside China GDP at 4.5% (down from 5%) is the same contradiction in a different geography — commodity markets are pricing a demand story that the underlying economies are not yet delivering. DXY at 98.09 (42nd percentile) and US 10Y at 4.42% (79th percentile, up 6bps) is the configuration that historically pressures EM capital flows — India is not immune.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity flows turned to -₹114 crore from +₹22,615 crore prior — a ₹22,729 crore reversal in a single period — while DII absorption was ₹128 crore, a rounding error against the outflow. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore (85th percentile) are structurally strong, but SIP account growth of 1.6% MoM to 10.45 crore (2nd percentile — weakest in a decade) reveals that the inflow is driven by rising ticket sizes from existing affluent investors, not new retail participation — the same cohort whose airline and e-commerce behaviour is already deteriorating. CD ratio at 83.04% with deposit growth at 10.8% means the banking system's internal liquidity is being stretched; corporate bond issuance at the 7th percentile signals that the credit rationing has already begun at the wholesale level. INR at 95.24 (89th percentile, near 52-week highs of weakness) with India VIX up to 18.46 from 17.44 suggests the market is beginning to price a risk premium that the equity indices have not yet fully reflected.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Nifty at 23,997 (77th percentile) and Bank Nifty at 54,863 (90th percentile) are both down on the session — Bank Nifty's premium valuation is the more exposed, given that CD ratio at 83.04% historically precedes either NIM compression or macro-prudential tightening via risk weights, both of which are earnings-negative for lenders. Real estate presents a structural contradiction: home loan rates fell to 8% from 9.1% and affordability improved to 0.61, yet launches sit at the 0th percentile (lowest ever) and sales fell to 1,01,675 units from 1,08,970 while unsold inventory rose to 6,00,000 units — developers are not launching into a market where 6 lakh units already sit unsold. Office absorption at 29.9mn sq ft with vacancy falling to 13.9% from 17.2% is the one clean positive in real assets — Bengaluru rents at ₹102/sq ft (up from ₹93.6) confirm commercial real estate is absorbing demand that residential is not. Equity risk is asymmetric: the bull case requires private capex to accelerate into a demand bifurcation; the bear case only requires the affluent consumer's retreat to broaden by one decile.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Three risks, ranked by probability × impact. First: demand bifurcation accelerates — airline traffic at a decade low and e-commerce GMV halved are leading indicators with a 2-3 quarter lag into GST and corporate earnings; if the affluent cohort's retreat broadens, the consumption narrative that is holding Nifty at the 77th percentile unravels. Second: CD ratio at 83.04% triggers a macro-prudential response — the RBI has used risk-weight increases on unsecured lending before (November 2023), and with NBFC credit growth already decelerating from 17% to 13%, a circular targeting specific segments would compress credit availability precisely when the consumption engine needs it. Third: Brent at $111.22 — down 5.8% — is providing a temporary CAD cushion, but at current prices India's annual oil import bill remains ₹8-9 lakh crore; any Middle East escalation that spikes Brent to $130+ widens CAD by 80-100bps and forces INR intervention at a moment when FII flows are already negative. Watch Friday's RBI liquidity data and next week's NBFC funding cost disclosures — those two prints will determine whether risk one and risk two are converging.
