India Macro Regime

Global Macro Regime

US GDP at 0.7% SAAR — down from 4.4% — while S&P 500 prints at an all-time high of 7,230 and US VIX sits at the 11th percentile is the most compressed risk premium in this cycle. US core PCE ticked up to 3.1% from 3.0%, keeping the Fed pinned at 3.625% with no cut in sight — yet the bond market is pricing US 10Y at 4.4%, down 2bps, as if growth will rescue inflation without policy action. China GDP at 4.5%, down from 5.0%, and Euro Zone GDP at 0.9%, down from 1.2%, confirm that global demand is softening synchronously — Brent's 5.2% single-session drop to $108 is the commodity market's honest assessment of where this leads.

Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net flows at -₹114 crore — a near-zero print after ₹22,615 crore of inflows last period — mark the sharpest single-period reversal in the data set, and INR at 95.24 (89th percentile, weakest in cycle) is the FX market's verdict. DII absorption at ₹128 crore is negligible — the domestic bid that cushioned every prior FII exit is absent. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 crore are still at the 85th percentile but have decelerated: YoY growth slipped to 15% from 17%, and SIP accounts at 10.45 crore sit at the 2nd percentile — the account base is thin relative to AUM, meaning average ticket sizes are large and concentrated, not broad-based retail depth.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Bank Nifty at 54,863 — 90th percentile — is pricing a banking sector whose CD ratio at 83.04% is at the 81st percentile and whose NBFC credit growth has already decelerated from 17% to 13%; the derating catalyst is a risk-weight circular, not a rate move. Residential real estate shows a clean affordability signal — home loan rates down to 8.0% from 9.1%, affordability index at 0.61 — but sales volumes fell to 101,675 units from 108,970 while launches accelerated to 126,265, pushing unsold inventory to 600,000 units. Office absorption at the 24th percentile with vacancy falling to 13.9% from 17.2% is the one commercial real estate signal that is genuinely improving — but retail mall vacancy rising to 5.7% from 5.1% says the consumer-facing segment is not.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Three risks, ranked by probability × impact: first, CD ratio at 83.04% forcing macro-prudential tightening — risk weights on unsecured lending would compress bank NIMs and trigger a Nifty Bank derating that passive funds cannot absorb without retail SIP support, which is itself decelerating. Second, Brent stabilising above $100 — today's 5.2% drop to $108 is still $8 above the threshold where every $10 adds ~$15 billion to India's import bill, widens CAD by 40bps, and feeds CPI with a two-quarter lag — precisely when RBI's inflation forecast of 4.6% leaves no room for a cut. Third, INR at 95.24 with FII flows near zero: if DXY recovers from 98.2 toward 101, the carry trade unwinds and India's $680 billion reserve cushion faces its first real test of the cycle. Watch Friday's RBI liquidity data — if the system deficit widens past ₹1.5 lakh crore, the deposit war begins in earnest.
