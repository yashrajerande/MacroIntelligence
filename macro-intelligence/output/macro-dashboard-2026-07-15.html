Executive Summary

01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP growth sits atop a consumption split that the aggregate cannot hide: PV sales at +26.1% and 2W sales at +28.7% are EMI-financed mass-market demand, while airline passengers fell 9.1% MoM to a 7th-percentile low and e-commerce GMV growth halved to 12.4% — the affluent discretionary layer is contracting. Core sector at 2.3% YoY — down from 4.7%, the weakest in 18 months — contradicts the IIP capital goods print of 12.5%; physical input volumes do not lie the way survey-based indices can. PMI Composite at 56.5 is still above 50, but the direction — down from 58.9 — matters more than the level when three sub-indices are decelerating simultaneously.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP at 0.7% SAAR — down from 4.4% — is the single most underappreciated number in global macro right now: the Fed is holding at 3.625% while core PCE ticks up to 3.1%, leaving real rates positive enough to keep the dollar sticky and EM carry trades under pressure. Euro Stoxx 50 at a 100th-percentile high and Nikkei at 67,744 — also a 100th-percentile print — suggest developed market equities are pricing a soft landing that US growth data does not yet support. China GDP at 4.5% with CPI at 1.3% — up from 0.2% — is the one constructive global signal: reflation without overheating, and iron ore's 65% surge to ₹161.91 is the commodity market's bet on Beijing's stimulus follow-through. Global PMI Composite at 51 — down from 51.5 — is barely expansionary; the world is not in recession, but it is not accelerating either.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net at -₹114 cr — a near-complete reversal from +₹22,615 cr the prior period — is India-specific, not dollar-driven: DXY at 100.94 is a neutral 52nd-percentile print, yet INR sits at 96.15, a 91st-percentile weakness. DII net inflows of ₹128 cr are negligible as an offset, and SIP inflows at ₹29,845 cr — down from ₹31,002 cr — are the structural floor, but SIP account growth at a 2nd-percentile low of 1.6% MoM signals the retail participation engine is not adding new entrants at the pace needed to absorb institutional selling. Corporate bond issuance at ₹1,12,000 cr — a 7th-percentile print — confirms that large borrowers are routing around the banking system, which tightens the credit channel for everyone below investment grade.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Bank Nifty at 57,462 — a 95th-percentile print — is pricing a banking sector whose CD ratio at 83.04% is at an 81st-percentile stress level; that divergence between price and funding reality has a historical resolution that is not flattering for bank multiples. Real estate shows its own bifurcation: residential sales fell to 1,01,675 units from 1,08,970 while launches rose to 1,26,265 units — unsold inventory has crossed 6,00,000 units, a supply overhang that the 8% average home loan rate (down from 9.1%) is only partially offsetting. Office absorption at 29.9 msf with vacancy falling to 13.9% from 17.2% is the one clean positive — commercial real estate is tightening while residential loosens. REIT prices tell the same story in reverse: Embassy, Mindspace, and Brookfield are all trading well below prior levels, suggesting the market has already discounted the residential overhang into the sector.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Three risks, ranked by probability times impact: first, the consumption bifurcation deepens — if airline traffic at a 7th-percentile low and e-commerce GMV at 12.4% growth are leading indicators of affluent spending fatigue, the GST and vehicle sales strength will lag by one to two quarters, and consensus FY27 earnings estimates carry 8-10% downside. Second, Brent at $85.45 — up 4 of the last 5 periods — crosses $90: every $10 adds roughly $15bn to India's import bill, widens CAD by 40bps, and puts the INR — already at a 91st-percentile weakness — under pressure that $680bn in reserves can buffer but not eliminate. Third, the CD ratio at 83.04% triggers a macro-prudential response — risk weight increases on unsecured lending or a deposit rate floor — that compresses NIMs and forces a Bank Nifty derating from its current 95th-percentile valuation. Watch Friday's RBI weekly credit-deposit data release: if the gap widens beyond 300bps for a fourth consecutive period, the probability of a regulatory intervention before the August policy meeting rises sharply.
