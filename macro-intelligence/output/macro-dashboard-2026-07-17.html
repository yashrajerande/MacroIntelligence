01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP growth sits alongside a core sector print of 2.3% YoY — a 240bps deceleration — and the gap between these two numbers is where India's macro story actually lives. PMI Composite at 56.5 is sliding from 58.9, with manufacturing at 53.9 (down from 56.9) suggesting the expansion is narrowing rather than broadening. Airline passenger traffic at a 7th-percentile low and e-commerce GMV growth collapsing from 25% to 12.4% in one period are the high-frequency signals that the PMI's backward-looking survey hasn't yet absorbed. CPI at 3.21% — up 46bps from prior — with the RBI revising its own inflation forecast to 4.6% means the rate-cut window is narrowing precisely as growth momentum peaks.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP at 0.7% SAAR — down from 4.4% prior — is the single most important number in today's global data set, and equity markets at all-time highs across three continents have not priced it. S&P 500 at the 100th percentile, Euro Stoxx 50 at the 100th percentile, and Nikkei at the 100th percentile while US core PCE ticks up to 3.1% from 3.0% — the Fed is not cutting into this, and the market's assumption that it will is the load-bearing wall of the current rally. China GDP at 4.5% (down from 5.0%) with iron ore spiking 64% to $161.91 is a commodity market pricing a stimulus that the underlying economy hasn't yet delivered. Global PMI Composite at 51.0 — the 49th percentile — is the honest read; everything else is multiple expansion on hope.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net flows flipped from +₹22,615 cr to -₹114 cr in a single period — the sharpest reversal in the dataset — while DII absorption at +₹128 cr (22nd percentile) is too thin to provide a meaningful cushion. INR at 96.15 — the 91st percentile — oscillating in a 180bps range over six periods suggests RBI intervention is capping depreciation, but the intervention itself draws down reserves that backstop the current account. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 cr are holding above the ₹29,000 cr floor, but SIP account growth at just 1.6% (2nd percentile) means the inflow resilience is entirely a function of rising ticket sizes among existing investors — a narrowing base, not a broadening one. Corporate bond issuance at ₹1,12,000 cr (7th percentile historically elevated) signals that credit rationing by banks is already forcing the real economy to find alternative funding at wider spreads.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Bank Nifty at the 95th percentile with CD ratio at 83.04% and NBFC credit growth decelerating from 17% to 13% is a valuation that assumes the funding stress resolves itself — history suggests it resolves via macro-prudential tightening instead. All three listed REITs are trading well below prior levels — Embassy at 369.92 vs 443.88, Mindspace at 345.06 vs 492.69, Brookfield at 263.49 vs 340.17 — even as office vacancy falls to 13.9% and Bengaluru rents rise to ₹102/sqft; the REIT discount to fundamentals is a liquidity story, not an asset quality story. Residential real estate shows a structural tension: launches at 1,26,265 units exceed sales at 1,01,675 units, pushing unsold inventory to 6,00,000 units — the affordability index improvement to 0.61 from 0.46 is real, but it hasn't yet cleared the supply overhang. Home loan rates at 8.0% are transmitting, but any CPI surprise that delays further cuts will stall the affordability recovery mid-cycle.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Brent at $85.05 — rising five consecutive periods from $76 — is the highest-probability near-term risk: every $10 adds approximately $15bn to India's import bill, widens CAD by 40bps, and pushes fuel inflation from -3.78% to positive within 4-6 weeks, forcing the RBI to choose between its pause and its credibility. Second, the CD ratio at 83.04% with deposit growth at 10.8% trailing credit at 13.8% is a structural constraint that tightens with every passing month — the risk is not a sudden crisis but a slow rationing of credit to NBFCs and mid-market corporates that shows up in IIP and GST data with a two-quarter lag. Third, US GDP at 0.7% SAAR against equity markets at all-time highs creates a binary: either the data is wrong and growth re-accelerates, or the multiple compression is violent when it comes — India's FII flow reversal from +₹22,615 cr to -₹114 cr in one period is the early tremor. Watch Friday's US core PCE revision — at 3.1% and rising, a further uptick closes the Fed's cutting window entirely and reprices every carry trade that has kept EM flows constructive.
