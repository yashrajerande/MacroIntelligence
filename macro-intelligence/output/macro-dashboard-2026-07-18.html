Executive Summary
▾
01
India Macro Regime

7.8% GDP with a 56.5 PMI Composite is the kind of data that looks unambiguously strong until you decompose it — Core Sector at 2.3% YoY, down from 4.7%, is the honest read on physical output, and it is moving in the wrong direction. Airline passengers at a 7th-percentile low and e-commerce GMV growth at 12.4% — half its prior reading — signal that urban discretionary demand is already softening, even as vehicle sales and GST collections hold. GST at ₹2,00,640 cr captures formal-economy activity and some formalisation reclassification; it does not capture the discretionary retrenchment visible in travel and online retail. India's macro regime is expansion on the surface, bifurcation underneath — and bifurcations of this kind have historically resolved downward, not upward.

02
Global Macro Regime

US GDP at 0.7% SAAR — down from 4.4% — is the single most underappreciated number in global markets, which are still priced at 100th-percentile valuations on the S&P 500 and Nikkei. Nikkei fell 4% in two sessions from a 10-year high, Euro Stoxx dropped 52bps — both at 100th-percentile valuations retreating simultaneously — which is carry-trade unwinding, not sector rotation. US 10Y at 4.57% — an 82nd-percentile print, rising — with US Core PCE at 3.1% (up from 3.0%) leaves the Fed with no credible path to cuts, even as growth decelerates to near-stall speed. Global PMI Composite at 51.0 — barely above contraction — with China GDP at 4.5% and Eurozone GDP at 0.9% confirms the soft-landing narrative is a lagging story, not a forward one.

03
Liquidity Conditions

FII equity net flows swung from +₹22,615 cr to -₹114 cr — a ₹22,729 cr reversal in a single month — while DII absorption at ₹128 cr is at the 22nd percentile, nowhere near sufficient to provide a floor. SIP inflows at ₹29,845 cr are down from ₹31,002 cr, and SIP account growth at a 2nd-percentile low confirms the retail participation engine is losing momentum even as AUM grows — the remaining inflows are increasingly concentrated in affluent ticket sizes, not broad-based. Corporate bond issuance at ₹1,12,000 cr — a 7th-percentile reading — signals large corporates are pricing banks out and accessing markets directly, which tightens the funding environment for mid-market borrowers who cannot. INR at 96.15 — 91st percentile — has not moved despite the FII reversal, but that stability is borrowed time if US yields stay above 4.5% and carry trades continue unwinding.

04
Equity + Real Estate Implications

Bank Nifty at 58,521 — 97th percentile — is pricing a banking sector whose CD ratio just hit 83.04%, NBFC credit growth has decelerated 400bps to 13%, and FII flows have just turned sharply negative; that is a valuation-to-fundamentals gap that macro-prudential tightening — via risk weights on unsecured lending — could close abruptly. Real estate tells a more nuanced story: office vacancy fell to 13.9% from 17.2%, Bengaluru rents rose to ₹102 from ₹93.6, and home loan rates dropped to 8% from 9.1% — the commercial and residential fundamentals are genuinely improving. Yet REIT prices tell the opposite story — Embassy down 17%, Mindspace down 30%, Brookfield down 22% — suggesting the rate-sensitive valuation discount is overwhelming the operational improvement. Unsold residential inventory at 6,00,000 units with launches outpacing sales (1,26,265 vs 1,01,675) is the supply overhang that will cap price appreciation even as affordability improves.

05
Key Risks to Monitor

Three risks, ranked by probability times impact: first, a carry-trade unwind accelerating — Nikkei and Euro Stoxx at 100th-percentile valuations retreating simultaneously, US 10Y at 4.57% and rising, with INR at a 91st-percentile level that has absorbed no depreciation yet — the pressure is building, not releasing. Second, food inflation persistence — CFPI at 3.47% from 2.13% in one month, FAO Food Index at 128.5 from 125.3, and Brent at $88.09 narrowing the fuel deflation buffer — the RBI's 4.6% inflation forecast may prove optimistic by Q3. Third, the consumption bifurcation deepening — airline pax at a 7th-percentile low and e-commerce GMV at 12.4% are leading indicators; if urban discretionary retrenchment spreads to mass-market categories currently holding (two-wheelers, CVs), the GDP growth trajectory reverts toward the RBI's own 6.9% forecast, not the 7.8% headline. Watch US 10Y at the next Fed communication — that is the single number that determines whether this week's carry-trade tremor becomes a dislocation.
